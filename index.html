🧭 Process — validate channels, log decisions, enforce rules
Every information channel is backtested against what it claims to predict (Median Absolute Error / hit rate) and only trusted channels (≥80% accuracy) may drive decisions. Every recommendation change is logged with its trigger and reviewed against the +30/+90-day outcome. Full write-up: PROCESS.md in the repo.

📏 Hard rules (override any score)
Earnings
Close or reduce single-stock positions before earnings — binary event, elevated volatility — even when the alt-data is favorable.
Hedging
Express sector risk via ETFs (XME metals, KARS EV) over adding single-name event risk; accept imperfect coverage.
Leading indicators
HRC price leads steel equities: HRC strength ⇒ cover / avoid shorts on domestic mills (CLF, STLD, NUE).
Insider asymmetry
Open-market insider buys always count; sells only when discretionary — RSU / plan sales are noise.
Micro overrides
Macro bias sets the default direction; a contradicting micro call is allowed but is flagged in the log until resolved.

Append-only audit trail (data/orders.jsonl, written by scripts/order_log.py from the /trade workflow). CREATED = instruction awaiting the owner's submission — open the IBKR link on a phone with the IBKR app to review & submit; the link errors in a plain desktop browser without an IBKR session. Statuses are updated by sessions as instructions are submitted, filled or cancelled. Trigger = what prompted the ticket (owner ask, a Stock-Picks recommendation, a Rebalance row, or an alert) and Why = the owner's rationale — the execution-side mirror of the decision log, so the review can ask "was the trigger right?". Claude prepares, the owner executes.
